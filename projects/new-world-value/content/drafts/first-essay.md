## Why Old Values Are Failing

For 30 years, the formula was simple:

**Stable job + Mortgage + Career ladder = Security + Meaning + Success**

That formula assumed three things:
1. Energy and supply chains would remain stable
2. Jobs would reward loyalty and tenure
3. Institutions (media, government, employers) could be trusted

All three assumptions are breaking — simultaneously.

| Trigger | What Changed | Why It Matters |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Geopolitical shocks** | Hormuz crisis → LNG down 40% → electricity tariffs up 8% | Energy is no longer guaranteed. Your grid is a single point of failure. |
| **AI + automation** | HK job vacancies down 55% YoY; 12,000 tech layoffs in Q1 2026 | Loyalty no longer protects you. Skills have a half-life of 18 months. |
| **Institutional trust erosion** | Trust in media: 22%. Trust in government: 34%. | You can't outsource verification anymore. The signal is yours to filter. |
| **Asset inflation** | HK home price-to-income: 23.2x (world's least affordable) | The ladder has been pulled up. Home ownership is no longer a milestone — it's a luxury. |
| **Systemic uncertainty** | World Uncertainty Index: 106,862 (all-time high) | Not one crisis — interdependence failure. Everything affects everything. |

This isn't a downturn. It's a **recalibration**.

And when the system recalibrates, your values must too.

## The Five Values of the New Era

Out of this recalibration, five values are emerging — not as abstract ideals, but as **observable behaviors**. People aren't talking about them. They're living them.

| Value | Old Definition | New Definition | Trigger |
|-------|----------------|----------------|---------|
| **Security** | Home ownership, stable job, savings account | Energy resilience, learning agility, exit options | Geopolitical shocks, AI layoffs, asset inflation |
| **Meaning** | Career ladder, status, titles | Enough, autonomy, boundaries, non-negotiables | Burnout, disillusionment, "quiet quitting" |
| **Autonomy** | Financial independence, early retirement (FIRE) | Control over time, attention, energy — right now | Remote work, AI leverage, geographic arbitrage |
| **Clarity** | Expert opinions, news consumption, credentials | Personal verification, signal filtering, inner compass | Information overload, trust erosion, algorithmic noise |
| **Resilience** | Emergency fund, insurance, backup plans | Adaptive capacity, local networks, skill redundancy | Supply chain shocks, institutional fragility |

These aren't replacements. They're **upgrades** — values that actually protect you in 2026, not in 1996.

## This Week: Security

Each week, we'll explore one value in depth. Week 1 is **Security** — because without security, nothing else matters.

### The Old Security

**Stable job + Mortgage = Security**

Home ownership wasn't just an asset. It was a promise: play by the rules, climb the ladder, and you'll be safe.

In Hong Kong, that promise is breaking.

- Home ownership rate: Down to 47.5% (lowest in 15 years) — HK Census & Statistics Dept, 2025
- Young adults (25-34) owning homes: 12% — down from 18% in 2015

The ladder isn't just steep. It's been pulled up.

### The New Security

The three graduates in Sheung Wan understand something the old formula doesn't capture:

**Security = Energy resilience + Learning agility + Exit options**

| Old Security | New Security |
|--------------|--------------|
| Home ownership | Energy resilience (backup power, local storage, demand flexibility) |
| Stable job | Learning agility (speed of unlearning + retooling) |
| Single income | Multiple income streams (geographic arbitrage, remote options) |
| 30-year mortgage | 12-month runway + portable skills |
| Tied to one city | Exit options (visas, networks, remote income) |

This isn't philosophy. It's observable behavior.

People aren't giving up on security. They're redefining it — because the old version no longer protects them.
