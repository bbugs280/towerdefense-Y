## Week 2 Research Brief (Gold Question)

### Core Question
If gold is a hedge against dollar weakness, is it too late to buy at $4,600 per ounce?

### Three Pillars Structure

#### 1. What Is Actually Happening (Facts)
- Gold price trajectory: $2,000 (2023) → $3,000 (2024) → $4,000 (2025) → $4,600 (Apr 2026)
- Central bank gold purchases: 2022-2026 trends (China, Poland, Singapore, India)
- Dollar index (DXY) correlation with gold price over past 5 years
- Real interest rates vs. gold performance
- ETF flows into gold (GLD, IAU) — retail vs. institutional

**Key Sources to Check:**
- LBMA (London Bullion Market Association) price data
- World Gold Council reports (quarterly)
- Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) — real rates, DXY
- IMF COFER data — central bank reserves
- Bloomberg / Reuters — recent analyst commentary

#### 2. What Could Happen Next (Scenarios)

| Scenario | Probability | Gold Price Implication | Dollar Implication |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Soft landing** — dollar bleeds slowly, no crisis | 50% | Gold consolidates $4,200-4,800 | DXY 95-105 range |
| **Stagflation** — recession + persistent inflation | 30% | Gold rallies to $5,500-6,000 | DXY falls to 85-90 |
| **Hard crash** — loss of reserve status accelerates | 15% | Gold spikes to $7,000+ | DXY breaks below 80 |
| **Dollar resurgence** — Fed hikes, crisis elsewhere | 5% | Gold corrects to $3,500-3,800 | DXY rallies to 115+ |

#### 3. What You Can Do About It (Action Framework)

**Not financial advice — frameworks for thinking:**

| Investor Profile | Consider This | Risk |
|------------------|---------------|------|
| **Already holds gold (5-10% portfolio)** | Hold, don't chase. Rebalance if >15%. | Opportunity cost if gold consolidates |
| **No gold, worried about dollar** | Dollar-cost average: 1-2% per month over 6 months | Timing risk, but reduces regret |
| **All-in on dollars/cash** | Consider 5-10% allocation as insurance, not speculation | Volatility if dollar rebounds |
| **Looking for speculation** | Gold miners, streaming companies (higher beta) | Company-specific risk, not pure gold play |

**Key principle:** Gold is insurance, not a get-rich-quick bet. Size accordingly.

## Historical Precedents to Reference

| Period | Context | Gold Performance | Lesson |
|--------|---------|------------------|--------|
| 1971-1980 | End of Bretton Woods, oil shocks, high inflation | $35 → $850 (2,400% gain) | Fiat debasement = gold bull market |
| 1980-2000 | Volcker disinflation, dollar strength | $850 → $250 (70% decline) | Gold can underperform for decades |
| 2001-2011 | Dot-com crash, 9/11, GFC, QE | $250 → $1,900 (660% gain) | Crisis + loose money = gold rally |
| 2011-2015 | Recovery, Fed taper | $1,900 → $1,050 (45% decline) | Gold corrects when confidence returns |
| 2019-2026 | Pandemic, inflation, de-dollarization | $1,250 → $4,600 (268% gain) | Current cycle |

**Key insight:** Gold moves in multi-year cycles. Timing matters less than conviction and position sizing.
