# New World Value — Decision Document

## One-line summary
A human-led, AI-augmented signal service that maps geopolitical, economic, and human shifts defining 2026+ — delivering clear, Asia-grounded insight via Substack + WhatsApp + Telegram without ideology, hype, or personal attribution risk.

## Market size and demand evidence

### Newsletter/Substack Landscape (Asia-focused)
- **Substack global paid subs**: ~2M active paid subscribers across all newsletters (Substack, 2025)
- **Asia-focused geopolitics newsletters**: <10 with >10K subscribers (mostly US/EU based)
- **HK/Asia professional audience**: ~2.5M English-speaking professionals 25-45 (HK Census + regional data)
- **Paid newsletter willingness**: 12-18% of professionals pay for ≥1 newsletter (Reuters Institute, 2025)

### Signal/Analysis Demand Indicators
- **Information overload**: 73% of professionals say "too much noise, not enough signal" (BCG Global Risk Survey, Feb 2026)
- **Trust deficit**: Only 22% trust traditional media for geopolitical analysis (Edelman Trust Barometer, 2026)
- **WhatsApp/Telegram news consumption**: +47% YoY among 25-45 age group in HK/SG/MY (We Are Social, 2026)

## Competitive landscape

### Who exists
1. **Traditional media** (SCMP, Reuters Asia, FT Asia) — Broad, institutional, slower
2. **Specialist newsletters** (China-focused, finance-focused) — Narrow, expert, often academic
3. **Social media analysts** (Twitter/X, LinkedIn) — Fragmented, algorithm-dependent, attribution risk
4. **Podcast/YouTube commentators** — Time-intensive, personality-dependent, discoverability challenges

### Where they fail
- ❌ **Western lens on Asia** — Most geopolitics analysis is US/EU-based interpreting Asia
- ❌ **Either too broad or too narrow** — General news lacks depth; specialist content lacks accessibility
- ❌ **Time burden** — Professionals don't have 30 min/day to synthesize signals
- ❌ **Trust issues** — Institutional media declining; independent voices often ideological
- ❌ **Single-channel** — Email-only or social-only; no multi-channel presence

### What makes New World Value different
- ✅ **Asia-grounded, not Asia-watching** — Creator lives in HK, speaks the language of systems AND people
- ✅ **Multi-channel by design** — Substack (long-form) + WhatsApp (daily signal) + Telegram (archive)
- ✅ **Human + AI workflow** — AI drafts, human curates — sustainable cadence without burnout
- ✅ **Zero attribution risk** — No face, no name, no employer reference — fully deniable
- ✅ **Values-first framing** — Not "what happened" but "what should you focus on" — action-oriented

## Key risks (top 3)

1. **Audience acquisition (cold start)** — No existing audience. First 100 subs hardest.
   → *Mitigation*: Leverage existing networks (WhatsApp contacts, LinkedIn). Cross-post to relevant communities. Partner with complementary newsletters.

2. **Time commitment vs. sustainability** — Weekly cadence = ~5-8 hrs/week (research, draft, curate, distribute).
   → *Mitigation*: AI-first drafting. Hook matrix = pre-planned content. Batch production (2-3 essays/session).

3. **Regulatory sensitivity (HK context)** — Geopolitical content in HK carries perception risk.
   → *Mitigation*: Public data only. No employer reference. Fully deniable brand. No financial/legal/regulatory claims.
